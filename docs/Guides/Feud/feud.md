# Feud of the Faithful XP Maximization

## TL;DR

To maximize XP in Feud of the Faithful, exploit how most of the peaceful route’s XP is awarded before the quest's formal completion. This allows you to double-dip by resolving the quest peacefully for dialogue XP, then killing Hulrun or Ramien for combat XP, and still turning in the quest to the surviving party for even more XP.

Optimal routes look like this:

- Convince Hulrun peacefully (gain most quest XP up front)

- Then kill Hulrun or Ramien in a way that doesn't sabotage Starward Gaze

- Then report Hulrun's or Ramien's death to the surviving party to finish the quest

This yields:

- Most of peaceful route XP

- Combat XP from killing Hulrun or Ramien

- Turn-in XP for reporting Hulrun or Ramien's death

- Full Starward Gaze XP if done right

If you only want the final, step-by-step optimised routes, you can skip ahead to the [Peaceful → Kill Routes](#Peaceful--Kill-Routes) section.

## Intro

This guide covers how to maximize experience points (XP) from the *Feud of the Faithful* questline.

In doing so it also covers the quest: *Starward Gaze*, which is closely intertwined with *Feud of the Faithful*.

The guide lists available routes, their XP yields, and identifies the most efficient ones, including recommendations for parties with changing sizes, which can affect XP distribution.

For the latter, also see my [Deep Dive: Shared Experience](https://youtu.be/T-W8uqmVQHg) and [Deep Dive: Shared Experience Addendum](https://youtu.be/iWK71YNBFvg) videos.


## Effect on playthrough

Before focusing on XP optimization, we’ll look at how *Feud of the Faithful* influences the broader playthrough.


### Main Outcomes of the Feud of the Faithful

- Ramien is Dead

- Hulrun is Dead

- Ramien and Hulrun make Peace


### Consequences for Act 1

#### *Starward Gaze*

- Killing **Ramien** before talking to **Ilkes** fails *Starward Gaze*.
- Killing **Ramien** prevents you from completing the quest by finding the Adepts.
- Completing the quest by finding the Adepts locks you out of killing Ramien.

#### *A Common Cause*

- If you **kill Hulrun**, you can recruit **Clerics of Desna** for *A Common Cause*.  They provide minor support throughout the Gray Garrison; they put some demons to sleep and cleanse a desecrated fountain (saves you a lore religion check). 
- If you **kill Ramien**, you can recruit **Prelate Hulrun's soldiers** for *A Common Cause*. They join in during an otherwise hard mini-boss fight. 
- If **Ramien and Hulrun reconcile**, you can recruit either one (but not both) for *A Common Cause*. 
- If you **kill both Ramien and Hulrun**, you can still complete *A Common Cause* (460 XP) by recruiting the Thieflings and/or the Knights of the Flame Lance. This quest completes or fails automatically just before entering the Gray Garrison.

### Long-Term Consequences

#### Act 3

- Completing *Starward Gaze* by helping the Adepts unlocks the **Azata Mythic Path** and allows for early recruitment of **Arueshalae**.

#### Act 5

(Details outside spoiler tags are intentionally sparse.)

- **Hulrun** and/or **Ramien** will show up in Act 5 if alive.
- **Hulrun** is useful in a specific Act 5 event. If you're Aeon a certain different character can take the same role. If you're Trickster you can ignore certain consequences.
??? note "Spoiler"
    Hulrun can protect the Sword of Valor during Heart of the Fallen Land, allowing you to do another objective first. Aeon can have Staunton Vhane protect it as well. Trickster has a special option on failure that I'm avoiding spoiling myself on. 
-  **Killing both** introduces a new character that takes the place of Hulrun and fullfills the same exact role in the aforementioned event (and in general).
??? note "Spoiler"
    If both die, Chun Dawei takes Hulrun's role in most things, including protecting the Sword of Valor during Heart of the Fallen Land. 
- **Ramien** is relevant in a separate Act 5 event; if you selected the Trickster dialog option for Nura in Act 2, she can help reach the same outcome.
??? note "Spoiler"
    During *Cascade of Fire*, Ramien will save the Stargazers if you decide to *stop Mutasafen*, but at the cost of his own life. If you decide to *save the Stargazers*, Nurah (if converted to your side) will stop Mutasafen from escaping. 
- You may offer either Ramien or Hulrun to the Cannibal in Act 5, provided at least one is still alive.

## Peaceful → Kill Routes 

You gain the bulk of the *Peaceful Feud of the Faithful* route not after completion, but at an earlier point, namely when you convince Hulrun.
This allows us to do a mixed approach, were we first convince Hulrun, get the majority of the XP, but then deviate and kill either Hulrun or Ramien instead, gaining the combat XP and turn-in XP from that route as well.

| Location/Event | Type | Detail | XP |

| **Total XP** | | | **1869** |
| Kill Hulrun (Optional) | Combat | Kill Hulrun and Inquisitors | 216 |
| **With Optional Kill** | | | **2085** |

Once you kill Ilkes, Ramien will become hostile if you speak to him again. If Ramien dies, you can obviously no longer convince Hulrun to make peace with him.

This means:

- You must speak with Ramien before killing Ilkes.

- You must report Ilkes’s death to Hulrun before approaching Ramien again.

This route normally provides slightly less XP than the Peaceful → Kill Hulrun route, but it can be completed entirely within Market Square.

As a result:

- It can yield more XP if playing reduced party then recruiting Nenio. 

- It get's XP slightly earlier in the act, which can mean reaching key power spikes earlier.

Finally if you kill Hulrun at the end it's the most XP route regardless of any of the above.
